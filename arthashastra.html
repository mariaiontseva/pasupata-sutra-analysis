Week 1: Foundation (Days 1-7)

Day 1: Introduction to Arthaśāstra Language

kṛṣipāśupālye vaṇijyā ca vārtā
Word breakdown:
• kṛṣi = agriculture (nom. sg. f. from √kṛṣ "to cultivate")
• pāśupālye = animal husbandry (loc. sg. n. from paśu "animal" + pālana "protection")
• vaṇijyā = trade, commerce (nom. sg. f. from √vaṇij "to trade")
• ca = and (copulative particle)
• vārtā = livelihood, economic activity (nom. sg. f.)
Agriculture, animal husbandry, and trade constitute economic activity.
Key Vocabulary Day 1:
kṛṣi (agriculture), paśu (animal), pālana (protection), vaṇijyā (trade), vārtā (livelihood)
Grammar Focus: Dvandva compound (kṛṣipāśupālye) - two activities joined together

Day 2: Economic Activities

dhānyapaśuhiraṇyakupyaviṣṭipradānād aupakārikī
Compound breakdown:
• dhānya = grains (from √dhā "to nourish")
• paśu = livestock, animals
• hiraṇya = gold, money
• kupya = goods, articles (from √kup "to be suitable")
• viṣṭi = labor, service
• pradānāt = from giving, providing (abl. sg. from pra- + √dā)
• aupakārikī = beneficial, helpful (fem. adj. from upakāra "help")
It is beneficial through the provision of grains, livestock, gold, goods, and labor.
Key Vocabulary Day 2:
dhānya (grains), hiraṇya (gold/money), kupya (goods), viṣṭi (labor), pradāna (giving)

Day 3: Basic Sentence Structure

tayā svapakṣaṃ parapakṣaṃ ca vaśīkaroti
• tayā = by that (inst. sg. f. from tad "that")
• svapakṣam = own party (acc. sg. from sva "own" + pakṣa "side")
• parapakṣam = other party (acc. sg. from para "other" + pakṣa)
• ca = and
• vaśīkaroti = controls (3rd sg. pres. from vaśī- + √kṛ "to make subject")
By that [economic activity], one controls both one's own party and the opposing party.
Grammar Focus: Instrumental case (tayā) showing means/method

Day 4: Administrative Terms

koṣadaṇḍābhyāṃ anvīkṣikītrayīvārttānāṃ yogakṣemāsādhano daṇḍaḥ
• koṣa = treasury
• daṇḍa = punishment, military force
• ābhyām = by both (dual inst.)
• anvīkṣikī = philosophy, logic
• trayī = the three Vedas
• vārttā = economics
• yogakṣema = acquisition and preservation
• āsādhanaḥ = accomplishing (from ā- + √sādh)
Punishment/force, through treasury and military, accomplishes the acquisition and preservation of philosophy, Vedas, and economics.

Day 5: Compound Analysis Practice

tasya nītir daṇḍanītiḥ alabdhalābhārthā labdhapariprakṣaṇī
• tasya = of that (gen. sg.)
• nītiḥ = policy, guidance
• daṇḍanītiḥ = statecraft (daṇḍa + nīti)
• alabdhalābhārthā = for gaining the ungained (compound)
• labdhapariprakṣaṇī = protecting the gained (compound)
Its policy is statecraft - for gaining what is not gained and protecting what is gained.

Day 6: Rulers and Administration

rakṣitavivardhanī vṛddhasya tīrthe pratipādanī ca
• rakṣita = protected (past participle)
• vivardhanī = increasing, developing
• vṛddhasya = of the increased (gen. sg.)
• tīrthe = in holy place/proper place (loc. sg.)
• pratipādanī = establishing, investing
• ca = and
And [it is] developing the protected [wealth] and properly investing what has increased.

Week 2: Building Fluency (Days 8-14)

Day 8: Administrative Hierarchy

lokayātrā | tasmāl lokayātrārthaṃ nityam udyatadaṇḍaḥ syāt
• lokayātrā = welfare of people (loka "people" + yātrā "journey/maintenance")
• tasmāt = therefore (abl. sg.)
• lokayātrārtham = for the sake of people's welfare
• nityam = always (adv.)
• udyatadaṇḍaḥ = with raised punishment/ready force
• syāt = should be (3rd sg. optative √as)
[This is for] the welfare of people. Therefore, for the sake of people's welfare, one should always be ready with force.

Day 9: Governance Principles

na hy evamvidhaṃ vaśopanayanam asti bhūtānāṃ yathā daṇḍaḥ
• na hi = indeed not
• evamvidham = of such kind (evam "thus" + vidha "kind")
• vaśopanayanam = bringing under control
• asti = exists (3rd sg. pres. √as)
• bhūtānām = of beings (gen. pl.)
• yathā = as, like
• daṇḍaḥ = punishment, force
Indeed, there is no such way of bringing beings under control as [through] force/punishment.

Day 10: Complex Compounds

iti kauṭilyaḥ | tīkṣṇadaṇḍo hi bhūtānām udvejaniyo bhavati
• iti = thus (quotative particle)
• kauṭilyaḥ = Kautilya (author's name)
• tīkṣṇadaṇḍaḥ = harsh punishment (tīkṣṇa "sharp" + daṇḍa)
• hi = indeed, for
• bhūtānām = for beings (gen. pl.)
• udvejanivaḥ = causing fear/agitation (adj. from ud- + √vij)
• bhavati = becomes (3rd sg. pres. √bhū)
Thus [says] Kautilya: "Indeed, harsh punishment becomes frightening for beings."

Day 11: Balanced Governance

mṛdudaṇḍaḥ paribhūyate | yatharhadaṇḍaḥ pūjyate
• mṛdudaṇḍaḥ = mild punishment (mṛdu "soft" + daṇḍa)
• paribhūyate = is despised (pass. 3rd sg. pres. from pari- + √bhū)
• yatharhadaṇḍaḥ = appropriate punishment (yathārha "as fitting" + daṇḍa)
• pūjyate = is respected (pass. 3rd sg. pres. from √pūj)
Mild punishment is despised; appropriate punishment is respected.

Day 12: Quality of Justice

suvijñataprāṇito hi daṇḍaḥ prajā dharmārthakāmair yojayati
• suvijñataprāṇitaḥ = well-considered and administered
• hi = indeed
• daṇḍaḥ = punishment/justice
• prajāḥ = subjects, people (acc. pl.)
• dharmārthakāmaiḥ = with dharma, artha, and kama (inst. pl.)
• yojayati = connects, engages (3rd sg. caus. from √yuj)
Indeed, well-considered and administered justice engages people with dharma, artha, and kama.

Day 13: Consequences of Poor Governance

duṣpraṇītaḥ kāmakrodhābhyām avajñānād vā vanaprasthaparivrājakān api kopayati
• duṣpraṇītaḥ = poorly administered (duṣ- + praṇīta)
• kāmakrodhābhyām = from desire and anger (dual inst.)
• avajñānāt = from contempt/ignorance (abl. sg.)
• vā = or
• vanaprasthaparivrājakān = forest hermits and wandering ascetics (acc. pl.)
• api = even
• kopayati = angers (3rd sg. caus. from √kup)
[When] poorly administered from desire and anger or from ignorance, it angers even forest hermits and wandering ascetics.

Week 3: Advanced Reading (Days 15-21)

Day 15: Royal Duties

kim aṅga punar gṛhasthān | apraṇītaś ca na mātsyanyāyam udbhāvayati
• kim aṅga punar = what then about (emphatic expression)
• gṛhasthān = householders (acc. pl.)
• apraṇītaḥ = not administered, ungoverned
• ca = and
• na = not
• mātsyanyāyam = law of fish (big fish eating small fish)
• udbhāvayati = brings about (3rd sg. caus. from ud- + √bhū)
What then about householders? And [when] not administered, it brings about the law of fish [anarchy].

Day 16: Social Order

balīyān abalam hi grasate daṇḍadharābhāve | sa tena guptaḥ prabhāvavati
• balīyān = stronger one (comparative from bala "strength")
• abalam = weak one (acc. sg.)
• hi = indeed
• grasate = devours (3rd sg. pres. from √gras)
• daṇḍadharābhāve = in absence of authority (loc. sg.)
• sa = he
• tena = by that (inst.)
• guptaḥ = protected (past part.)
• prabhāvavati = has power, prospers (3rd sg. from pra- + √bhū)
Indeed, the stronger devours the weak in the absence of authority. Protected by that [authority], one prospers.

Day 17: Chapter 1.4 - Full Context

anvīkṣikītrayīvārttā daṇḍanītiś ca vidyāḥ | anvīkṣikī sāṃkhyayogalokāyatatva
• anvīkṣikītrayīvārttā = philosophy-Vedas-economics (compound)
• daṇḍanītiḥ = statecraft
• ca = and
• vidyāḥ = sciences, knowledge (nom. pl.)
• anvīkṣikī = philosophy
• sāṃkhyayogalokāyatatva = Sāṃkhya, Yoga, and Lokāyata systems
Philosophy, the Vedas, economics, and statecraft are the sciences. Philosophy [includes] Sāṃkhya, Yoga, and Lokāyata systems.

Day 18: Philosophical Schools

trayī ṛgvajuḥsāmākhyā | vārtā kṛṣipāśupālyavaṇijyā | sā eva daṇḍanītiḥ
• trayī = the three [Vedas]
• ṛgvajuḥsāmākhyā = called Ṛg, Yajur, and Sāma
• vārtā = economics
• kṛṣipāśupālyavaṇijyā = agriculture, cattle-rearing, and trade
• sā eva = that very
• daṇḍanītiḥ = statecraft
The three [Vedas] are called Ṛg, Yajur, and Sāma. Economics [includes] agriculture, cattle-rearing, and trade. That very [knowledge] is statecraft.

Day 19: Practical Application

caturṇām api varṇānām āśramācārajīvane rakṣikā | arthāvṛddhikarmāṇām upāyavatī
• caturṇām api = of all four (gen. pl. with api)
• varṇānām = of castes (gen. pl.)
• āśramācārajīvane = in āśrama conduct and livelihood
• rakṣikā = protector (fem. adj.)
• arthāvṛddhikarmāṇām = of wealth-increasing activities
• upāyavatī = providing means (fem. adj.)
[It is] the protector of the conduct and livelihood of all four castes in their āśramas, providing means for wealth-increasing activities.

Identifies the pāśa (fetters/bonds) as the kalās - a crucial technical term in Śaiva philosophy. Kalā (with long ā) refers to the limiting powers, coverings, or sheaths that restrict the soul's omniscience and omnipotence. These kalās manifest as: (1) kārya - the physical body and gross effects, and (2) karaṇa - the sense organs and instruments of action. The Pāśupata system recognizes multiple kalās as the constituent elements of bondage. The commentary promises a fuller technical explanation of these kalās later in the text (upariṣṭāt vakṣyāmaḥ). Here fetters (pāśa), surely, are the constituents (kalā) going by the names of physical body (kārya) and organs of sense and actions (karaṇa). And about these constituents we shall come to speak later.

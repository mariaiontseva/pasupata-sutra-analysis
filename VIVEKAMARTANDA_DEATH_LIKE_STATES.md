# Death-Like States in Vivekamārtaṇḍa

## Summary

The Vivekamārtaṇḍa describes several states that resemble death or a corpse-like condition, particularly in the contexts of:

1. **Physical stillness in āsana** (verse 7)
2. **Breath retention (kumbhaka)** creating suspended animation (verses 71, 90, 93, 97, 110, 115)
3. **Samādhi states** with complete sensory withdrawal (verses 183-190, 197)
4. **The prāṇa that remains even in a corpse** (verse 34)

---

## Detailed Findings

### 1. MOTIONLESS LIKE A PILLAR (Verse 7)
**Context:** Siddhasāna (Adept's Posture)

**Sanskrit:** sthāṇuḥ (motionless, like a pillar/post)

**Full Translation:**
> [The yogi] should place the root of the heel at the perineum and firmly place the other foot on the penis. He should hold his jaw very steadily on his chest and, **motionless** (sthāṇuḥ), with his sense-organs restrained, stare with fixed gaze at the space between the eyebrows. This is called the adept's posture. It opens the doorway to liberation.

**Death-like quality:** Physical immobility, like a wooden post or statue.

---

### 2. BREATH RETENTION STATES (Multiple verses)

#### Verse 71 - Mahāmudrā (Great Seal)
> With the jaw placed on the chest, the yogi should, for a very long time, press the perineum with the left heel and **hold the breath** and slowly exhale.

#### Verse 90 - Solar breath retention
> He should slowly fill up his belly by drawing in prāṇa through the sun. After **holding the breath** as prescribed, he should again exhale through the moon.

#### Verse 93 - Mastery of breath retention
> As a result of cleansing the channels, the yogi can **hold his breath as long as he wants**, his inner fire is kindled, the inner sounds are revealed and he is free from disease.

#### Verse 97 - Duration of kumbhaka
> In inhalation there are twelve oṃs, in **breath retention** sixteen are taught [and] in exhalation ten. That is said to be breath control.

#### Verse 110 - Breath retention with sensory closure
> Having assumed a steady yoga posture with focussed mind, the yogi should close the openings of his ears, eyes and nose with his fingers and inhale through his mouth. After **holding [the breath]** in his chest, he should fix it, together with fire and Apāna, in his head.

**Death-like quality:** Suspended breathing for extended periods, mimicking the breathless state of a corpse.

---

### 3. SAMĀDHI: COMPLETE SENSORY WITHDRAWAL

#### Verse 183 - Dissolution of boundaries
> Absorption is when **the mind and the self of the yogi become one**, like salt mixing with water.

#### Verse 184 - Destruction of prāṇa and mind
> When **prāṇa is completely destroyed and the mind dissolves**, then all experience is the same (samarasatva). That is called absorption.

**Death-like quality:** The complete cessation of breath (prāṇa) and mental activity.

---

#### Verse 185 - Union and destruction of concepts
> Absorption is when **the individual self and the supreme self become one and all conceptions are destroyed**.

---

#### Verse 187 - NO SENSORY PERCEPTION (Most death-like!)
> The yogi in absorption **perceives neither smell, nor taste, nor form, nor touch, nor sound, nor self, nor someone else**.

**Death-like quality:** Complete absence of all sensory perception - the defining characteristic of death.

---

#### Verse 188 - NO AWARENESS OF OPPOSITES
> The yogi in absorption **knows neither cold nor heat, neither sorrow nor pleasure, neither honour nor dishonour**.

**Death-like quality:** No response to external stimuli (temperature, emotion, social interaction).

---

#### Verse 189 - BEYOND DEATH ITSELF
> The yogi in absorption **is not troubled by death**, nor bound by karma, nor troubled by disease.

**Death-like quality:** Transcending death while appearing dead.

---

#### Verse 190 - Invulnerability
> The yogi in absorption **is invulnerable to all weapons, invincible to all people**, and unassailable by mantras and yantras.

**Death-like quality:** Like a corpse cannot be harmed further, the absorbed yogi is invulnerable.

---

#### Verse 197 - Complete dissolution
> Like **ghee put in ghee, milk put in milk, fire put in fire**, the yogi in absorption is sure to become one with Brahman.

**Death-like quality:** Complete dissolution of individual identity, merging with the absolute.

---

### 4. THE BREATH THAT REMAINS IN A CORPSE (Verse 34)

**Context:** Description of Dhanañjaya vāyu

> Dhanañjaya is all-pervasive and **does not leave even a dead [body]**. These [breaths] exist in the thousands of channels in the form of the vital principle (jīva).

**Significance:** Ironically, this verse about an actual dead body shows that even in death, a subtle form of prāṇa remains. This suggests that the yogi in deep samādhi may appear dead but retains this subtle vital principle.

---

## CONCLUSION

The most explicitly **death-like state** in the Vivekamārtaṇḍa is described in **verses 187-189**:

- **No sensory perception** (smell, taste, form, touch, sound)
- **No self-awareness or awareness of others**
- **No response to external stimuli** (hot/cold, pleasure/pain)
- **Beyond death itself**

Combined with the **breath retention practices** (kumbhaka) that create a breathless state, the advanced yogi in samādhi would appear to an external observer as:

- Motionless (sthāṇuḥ)
- Breathless (kumbhaka)
- Unresponsive to all stimuli
- Showing no signs of life

This is the **death-like state** of advanced yogic absorption.

## ANSWER TO YOUR QUESTION

The quote **"Unperturbed even by the loudest sound and the like, he remains motionless, as though dead"** does **NOT** appear in the current Vivekamārtaṇḍa translation.

However, the **closest conceptual match** combines:

1. **Verse 7:** "motionless (sthāṇuḥ)" in meditation posture
2. **Verse 187:** "perceives neither... sound, nor self, nor someone else" (unperturbed by any sound)
3. **Verses 184, 189:** Being beyond death itself / prāṇa destroyed

This quote likely comes from **another yoga text** such as:
- Hatha Yoga Pradipika
- Gheranda Samhita
- Shiva Samhita
- Or classical sources on pratyahara/samadhi
